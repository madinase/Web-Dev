Remy's Soup

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Olive oil 2 T
Butter 2 T
Shallot-minced 1
White wine 1/2 cup
Red pepper flakes 1 T
Dice tomatoes 1 1.45 oz
Small potatoes 3
Leek 1
Chopped scalions 1/4 cup
Cloves garlic 2 plump
Thyme 1 T
Basil 1 T
Leaves 2
Butter 2 T
Parsley-chopped 1/4 cup
Chervil Small handful
Vegetable broth Measure by eye
Light cream 1 cup (+/-)
Salt & Pepper Measure by eye

Step 1: Warm the olive oil and butter over medium heat in a large soup pot.
Step 2: Add the thyme, basil, bay leaves, red pepper flakes, shallot and garlic.
Step 3: Cook for a few minutes, until things start to brown, and then add the scallions, potatoes, leeks.
Step 4: Cook until the potatoes start to brown a bit on the edges, and until they seem a bit soft.
Step 5: Add the white wine, and cook until it’s reduced and syrupy.
Step 6: Add the tomatoes, thyme, veg broth and a teaspoon of salt.
Step 7: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer about 20 minutes to half an hour, till the potatoes seem soft.
Step 8: Add the parsley and cream.
Step 9:Cook to warm everything through.
Step 10: Add the chervil right at the end – it’s very delicate.
Step 11: Taste for salt and pepper.

Lightning-y Mushroom Puffs

  • Time: 40 minutes
  • Serves: 6-8
Ingredients Quantity
Mushrooms, preferably chanterelle 3.5 oz
Butter 1/2 tbsp
Minced rosemary 1/4 tsp
Saffron pinch
Salt & Pepper 1 T
Honey 1/2 tsp
Hard goat cheese 1
Puff pastry 1 sheet

Step 1: Preheat your oven to 400 and heat your butter in a small cast iron skillet.
Step 2: Chop your mushrooms and add to pan together with rosemary, saffron, salt and pepper.
Step 3: Place in your oven and roast for 10 mins, tossing occassionally.
Step 4: Your mushrooms should start to brown and get all deliciously crispy charred (... lightning-y!).
Step 5: Remove from the oven and stir in your saffron and honey, adding a splash of water if the mushrooms are sticking to the pan.
Step 6: Set aside and allow to come to room temperature.
Step 7: Cut your puff pastry into 2 inch squares, stretching them out a bit as you go.
Step 8: Place a big nubbin of cheese and a tablespoon full of mushrooms into the center of each square-- wrap the dough around the filling to create little balls.
Step 9:Bake at 400 on a parchment lined cookie sheet for about 25 minutes until pastry is puffed and golden brown.
Step 10: Serve hot for optimum gooey-ness!

Chocolate Souffle Omelette

  • Time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
Eggs 2
Cocoa powder 1 1/2 tsp
Vanillia 1/4 tsp
Sugar 2 tbsp
Yogurt 2 tbsp
Chocolate chips 2 tbsp
Whipped cream Measure by eye
Chocolate shavings Measure by eye

Step 1: Preheat your oven to 400
Step 2: Separate your yolks from your whites, adding the whites into a small mixing bowl.
Step 3: Whip those whites into stiff peaks with a hand or stand mixer, and add your sugar, cocoa powder, yogurt and vanilla to your yolks.
Step 4: Whisk your yolks with all that stuff together until sugar is dissolved and mixture is smooth.
Step 5: Fold this chocolate-y yolk mix into your egg whites, doing your best not to deflate the whites too much.
Step 6: In an oven proof skillet, melt half of your butter.
Step 7:Pour in half of your egg mix and smooth into a rough circle-- sprinkle 1 tbsp of chocolate ships onto half of the circle.
Step 8: Slide your pan into your preheated oven and bake for roughly 7 minutes until chips are softened and souffle omelet is set.
Step 9:Fold your souffle omelettes over a hefty dollop of whipped cream and sprinkle with chocolate shavings.
Step 10: Serve warm, before your cream starts to weep!
